Grah (Bean soup)

A classic bean soup from the Balkans

Grav, grah, pasulj
This bean soup from the Balkans gets its lovely creaminess with the help of a rescue made from fried flour and paprika powder.

Ingredients

For 6 portions
  • white beans, dried 500g
  • Water 1,8dl
  • Salt 1 spoon
  • Yellow onion 1 piece
  • Sremska sausage 300g
  • Red pepper 1 piece
  • Vegeta 1 spoon
  • Salt and pepper
    • Roux
  • sunflower oil 0.5dl
  • Flour 3 spoons
  • paprika powder 3 spoons
    • For serving
  • Parsley
  • Bread
  • Feta cheese
  • Pepperoni

Do this

  1. Soak the beans overnight. Drain the water and put the beans in a pot.
    Top up with about 1.8 liters of new water and salt. The water should cover the beans.

  2. Peel and cut the onion in half and the sausage into thick slices.
    Split the pepper and remove the stem and core.

  3. Add onion, sausage and paprika to the pot.
    Bring to a boil and simmer with the lid on for about 40 minutes or until the beans are almost tender.
    The time depends on how big the beans are and how long they have been soaking.
    If foam forms at first, remove it with a spoon. Top up with more water if necessary.

  4. Pick up the cooked onion and pepper. Peel off the skin of the pepper and chop both vegetables.
    Put them back in the pot.

  5. Make the rescue. Heat the sunflower oil in a frying pan.
    Add the flour and let it sizzle while stirring. Remove from the plate and add the paprika powder.
    Stir to a thick consistency and pour into the pot. Add Vegeta.

  6. Cook for another 10 minutes uncovered. When the beans are soft, the soup is ready.
    If you want it thicker, add more flour. For a thinner soup, add more water.

  7. Season with salt and black pepper. Serve with parsley, bread, feta cheese and pepperoni.

    8. Done
